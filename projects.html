Semester Games

Since Fall 2020, students in the UNL Game Dev Club have worked in teams to develop games over the course of each semester. All of the completed games are listed below and are free to download or play online.

Spring 2022

Otto Bots

Battle a friend for control of an island sinking in the sand in Otto Bots, a local/online, asymmetric multiplayer strategy game!

Otto Bots was made by Josh Bellmyer (team lead), Danny Roland, Daniel Riedel, Say Wah, Paul Hennerich, Harry Strong, and Caleb Kirilov.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows and Mac.

Unfinished Business

Unfinished Business is a puzzle-platformer where you play as a ghost who is trying to pass on to the afterlife.

Unfinished Business was made by Eric Dundas (team lead), Bobby Spatz, Caleb Rosenboom, Sarah Lowderman, and Andrejs Sedriks.

The demo can be played online and downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Exocutioner

Exocutioner is a Doom-like first-person shooter, but with wall-walking gravity physics inspired by Super Mario Galaxy.

Exocutioner was made by Ivy Chobyan (team lead), Sam Woods, John Le, Ethan Schmidt, and Lance.

The demo can be downloaded from GitHub for Linux and Windows.

Globetrotter

Globetrotter is a 3D exploration videogame where players puzzle their way through levels on all seven continents in search of the ultimate treasure. Players will learn about the local culture, solve riddles, and decode hidden messages using different types of ciphers.

Globetrotter was made by Ellenna Divingnzzo.

UNL's Education Abroad office is looking forward to partnering with Ellenna to create an adaptation of Globetrotter for students travelling abroad.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Fall 2021

Things Go Bump in the Night

Can you guide Ted through the mines while avoiding its creature?

Things Go Bump in the Night was made by Ellenna Divingnzzo (team lead), Ethan Schmidt, Liam Seper, Dylan Wonder, and Junior Wills.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Tales from the Metahedron

Welcome to the Metahedron, where space is fractured and attacks don't have to happen on the game board!

Tales From the Metahedron was made by Sam Woods (team lead), Ivy Chobanyan, Wally Holbeck, Wesley Hill, and Logan Willis.

The team completed a demo over the course of the semester, but it is not publicly available.

Riot Relief

Riot Relief is a riot/protest simulator where you can purchase officers, vehicles, and equipment in order to protect an important objective from aggressive individuals breaking the law.

Riot Relief was made by Logan White (team lead) and Thadd Post.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Levels of Mystery

Levels of Mystery is a game where you play as an apartment building's elevator operator who must solve a murder mystery.

Levels of Mystery was made by Ben Laube (team lead), Trey Hestermann, Jacob Clouse, Alijah Mallula, Avery West, Carson Haberyan, and Jaevyn Hoyt.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Minieval

Confined

In Confined, explore the city of Sarin and delve into the mysteries as to what happened to the many citizens of this small town, all the while wondering if this town is still as dead as it may seem.

Confined was made by Zenith Boerger (team lead), Wally Holbeck, Tyler Senne, Alijah Mallula, and David.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io for Windows.

Giant's Conquest

In Giant's Conquest you are a giant in search of fame and power. You can accomplish your goals by capturing human villages and slaying the enemy before you!

Giant's Conquest was made by Samuel Ingledue (team lead), Ben Laube, Logan White, Chris Bayliss, Tristan Nord, and Jaevyn Hoyt.

Jam Games

Club members have competed in several game jams, which are competitions in which each individual or team makes a game in a short period of time (usually one or a few days).

UNL Game Dev Club Game Jam 2022

The UNL Game Dev Club hosted its first game jam on November 12-13, 2022. The jam ran for 34 hours.

At Large

In At Large, you are an escaped convict at large, and every 5 seconds you need to make sure you are hidden amongst the crowd. If you aren't 70% covered when the timer runs out your score is reset. How high can you get your score?

At Large was made by Ben Laube.

The game can be played online on itch.io.

Game Maker's Toolkit Game Jam 2020

Whiz Blade

In Whiz Blade, a top-down puzzler, you must escape a tower with a parasitic sword on your arm that can't help but kill whatever is in front of it!

Whiz Blade was made by Jaden Goter, Zach Randolph, Matt Lowe, Erik Skoog, and Camden Obertop.

The game placed within the top 2% of the 5,416 submissions. The jam was the largest online game jam ever at the time.

The game can be played online on itch.io. Its source code is available on GitHub.

Ludum Dare 46

Squatch Watch

In Squatch Watch, a PS1-style stealth game, you must play as Bigfoot in the forest leaving clues and sneaking past guards to keep the myth of the squatch alive!

Squatch Watch was made by Jaden Goter, Grant Davis, Matt Lowe, Erik Skoog, and Camden Obertop. All members were sophomores in Software Engineering.

The game placed within the top 25% of the 4,959 submissions. The jam was the largest online game jam ever at the time.

The game can be played online on itch.io.

Game Maker's Toolkit Game Jam 2019

1 Slot Wonder

In 1 Slot Wonder, a puzzle-platformer, you must use four unique items to navigate your way to the end of the level. The twist? You can only hold one item at a time, so you must throw the items around the level to transport them!

1 Slot Wonder was made by Jaden Goter.

The game placed within the top 5% of the 2,606 submissions. The jam was the largest online game jam ever at the time.

The game can be downloaded from itch.io. Its source code is available on GitHub.

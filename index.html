Welcome to the UNL Game Development Club

We are UNL's only club focused on learning to develop video games. We meet weekly on Sundays at 7 pm in Avery Hall. All are welcome and there are no pre-requisites to join.

Our mission: To teach students effective professional game design techniques. We accomplish this by working through game development projects in our meetings, such as demos or active projects. We also provide students the opportunity to showcase their skills at game development competitions ("game jams").

To get involved, please visit the Contact page and reach out to one of the club officers.

Upcoming Events

The GDC has some exciting events coming up, which we'd like to share with everyone!

Unity Demos (March 10th)

On March 10th), the club officers will begin hosting a series of Unity demos based on club interst. The options for these Unity demos include 2D, 3D, physics, collisions, graphics, animations, and more. As part of this series, the club officers will also be able to show outside tools that will help create content to use in Unity projects.

Please visit the Contact page and submit a recommendation form if you have a recommendation for topics to include in the Unity demos.

Spring Break (March 17th and March 24th)

The club will not have official meetings over Spring Break. Students who wish to work on their projects over break are encouraged to do so and coordinate with their teammates to organize efforts.

Unity Demos (March 31st)

The club will resume the series of Unity demos and continue to cover topics from the following areas: 2D, 3D, physics, collisions, graphics, animations, and outside tools to help create content.

Please visit the Contacts page and submit a recommendation form if you have a recommendation for topics to include in the Unity demos.
